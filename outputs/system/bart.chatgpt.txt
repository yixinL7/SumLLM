Filmmaker Michael König from Cologne, Germany has created an amazing video showing solar activity, which was made by stitching together footage from Nasa's Solar Dynamics Observatory over five years. The video includes plasma raining down on the sun, an extreme solar eruption and even a comet breezing through the sun’s atmosphere. By watching the sun in different wavelengths, scientists can watch how material courses through the corona, which holds clues to what causes eruptions on the solar surface, what heats the sun's atmosphere up to 1,000 times hotter than its surface, and why the Sun's magnetic fields are constantly on the move.
