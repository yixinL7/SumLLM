An incredible video has been created by filmmaker Michael König from Cologne, Germany, showing the beauty of the solar surface as it bursts with energy. The video, called Sun, was created by stitching together footage from Nasa's Solar Dynamics Observatory over five years, featuring plasma raining down on the sun, an extreme solar eruption, and even a comet breezing through the sun's atmosphere. By watching the sun in different wavelengths and temperatures, scientists can watch how material courses through the corona, which holds clues to what causes eruptions on the Sun, what heats its atmosphere up to 1,000 times hotter than its surface, and why its magnetic fields are constantly on the move.
