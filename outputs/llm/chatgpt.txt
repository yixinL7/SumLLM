A filmmaker from Cologne, Germany has created a stunning video called "Sun" by stitching together footage from NASA's Solar Dynamics Observatory over five years. The video shows solar activity such as plasma raining down on the sun, an extreme solar eruption, and a comet passing through the sun's atmosphere. By watching the sun in different wavelengths, scientists can understand how material courses through the corona, which holds clues to what causes eruptions on the sun, what heats the sun's atmosphere up to 1,000 times hotter than its surface, and why the sun's magnetic fields are constantly on the move.
